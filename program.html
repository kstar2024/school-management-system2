    • Programs Offered

    • Elementary School: Kindergarten through 5th Grade

      Our elementary program focuses on foundational skills in reading, writing, and mathematics. We also introduce students to science, social studies, and the arts.

      Kindergarten:

      Our kindergarten program lays the foundation for lifelong learning with a focus on social skills, literacy, and numeracy. Activities include storytelling, basic math concepts, art, and physical education.

      1st Grade:

      First graders develop their reading and writing skills, learn basic arithmetic, and explore introductory science and social studies topics. Hands-on activities and interactive lessons make learning fun and engaging.

      2nd Grade:

      Second grade focuses on enhancing reading fluency, writing structure, and math problem-solving skills. Students also participate in science experiments and learn about their community and the world.

      3rd Grade:

      Third graders deepen their comprehension and analytical skills in reading, tackle more complex math problems, and conduct simple scientific investigations. They also begin learning about history and geography.

      4th Grade:

      Fourth grade emphasizes critical thinking in all subject areas. Students read and analyze more challenging texts, master multiplication and division, and explore life sciences and American history.

      5th Grade:

      Fifth graders prepare for middle school with advanced reading and writing projects, more sophisticated math concepts including fractions and decimals, and in-depth studies of physical science and early American history.

    • Middle School: 6th Grade through 8th Grade

      Middle school students engage in a more rigorous curriculum, including advanced mathematics, science, and language arts. Extracurricular activities and sports are also a key part of the middle school experience.

    • High School: 9th Grade through 12th Grade

      Our high school program prepares students for college and careers with a wide range of courses, including honors and Advanced Placement options. Students can participate in various clubs, sports, and leadership opportunities.

    • Advanced Placement (AP): Courses for college credit

      AP courses offer college-level curricula and exams to high school students. Students can earn college credit and demonstrate their readiness for higher education.
